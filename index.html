A New Tool for Watershed Services

NatCap's newest software, Resource Investment Optimization System (RIOS), is a free and open source software tool that supports the design of cost-effective investments in watershed services. RIOS provides a standardized, science-based approach to watershed management. It combines biophysical, social, and economic data to help users identify the best locations for protection and restoration activities in order to maximize the ecological return on investment, within the bounds of what is socially and politically feasible.

Coastal Resilience 2.0

NatCap has been working with TNC to apply nature-baased solutions to coastal hazard and adaptation issues. The US Coastal Resilience tools we helped build, provide support for decision-makers working in the continental US identify solutions for risk reduction and conservation. They build from critical resources provided by many groups and agencies including NOAA, USGS, FEMA, USFWS, TNC and the Natural Capital Project.

Learn more about the Coastal Resilience Network and explore coastal resilience 2.0.


Making use of those vacation photos you posted online

NatCap's scientists are using social media to study how people use natural areas. We have found a way to use information from the photo-sharing site Flickr to measure where and when people are using natural areas for recreation and tourism. This information can help measure the benefits and value that these areas provide to society.

Read the story and download the paper in scientific reports.
