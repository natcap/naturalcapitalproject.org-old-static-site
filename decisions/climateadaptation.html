Coastal Defense & Climate Adaptation

Over half the world's population lives within 200km from a coast, and coastal hazards are increasing with climate change. In spite of this increasing vulnerability, no standardized approach or decision-support tool currently exists to support coastal hazard planning that includes protective services of coastal habitats.

The Natural Capital Project (NatCap) and partners are working with decision makers and coastal planners to assess the vulnerability of communities and infrastructure to climate change impacts, and to explore the range of adaptation strategies available to support resilient ecosystems and human communities.

NatCap uses ecosystem services information from InVEST to determine how future climate scenarios and adaptation strategies can affect the services provided by natural ecosystems, such as coastal protection, fisheries provision, and recreational opportunities and revenue. Scientists are using these results to inform national and regional climate adaptation and natural hazard mitigation planning.


Ecosystems as Natural Defenses

Coastal planners are grappling to develop management strategies that protect shorelines from rising sea levels, intensified erosion, and extreme storm events. NatCap's suite of InVEST tools provide approaches to link ecological change with the economic value of protective services provided by coastal habitats. Based on InVEST outputs, we support government and businesses to design cost-effective, ecosystem-based strategies to mitigate coastal impact, such as wetland and oyster reef restoration.


Applications

InVEST models can support government agencies, private corporations, and non-governmental organizations to:

  • Incorporate natural capital into climate adaptation strategies and integrated spatial management plans

  • Develop risk-management strategies by iterating habitat management scenarios and informaing specific decisions

  • Determine costs and benefits of different adaptation strategies by quantifying the contribution of ecosystem services to coastal security

Mapping risk reduction along the U.S. coast

We used InVEST to create the first United States map of risk-reduction due to coastal habitats, suggesting areas where investment in coastal eosystems will be critical to coastal defense and climate adaptation planning. This map suggests that 67% of the United States coast is protected by natural habitats such as wetlands and reefs. NatCap is working with local partners in California, the Gulf of Mexico, and the U.S. Northeast to apply the approach on smaller scales using local data.

Climate Scenarios in Belize

In Belize, NatCap worked with local stakeholders to develop realistic climate change scenarios along the coast, and to develop strategies that would mitigate impacts to ecosystem services. Scenarios reflected two different climate scenarios (inaction and mitigation) and two climate adaptation scenarios (integrated and reactive). Integrated climate adaptation strategies emphasized conservation and restoration of key coastal habitats. Reactive adaptation scenarios emphasized engineered strategies, such as seawalls.

InVEST software was used to value and visualize how ecosystem services, including coastal protection, lobster fisheries, tourism/recreation and carbon sequestration would respond to alternative climate and management scenarios. InVEST quantified and produced maps of ecosystem services for these different scenarios and determined the cost-benefit tradeoffs for different futures. These results have been a starting point for future analyses in Belize and other countries interested in applying these approaches to decisions about coastal planning and disaster risk.
