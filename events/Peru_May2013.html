WWF Peru InVEST Training


InVEST Training and Ecosystem Services Policy Workshop

May 27, 2013 - May 31, 2013
Lima, Peru

WWF Peru organized an InVEST training and policy workshop on ecosystem services in Lima, Peru in collaboration with Peru's Ministry of the Environment (MINAM) and the Living Amazon Initiative. The workshop built upon existing InVEST analyses and partnerships in the region as well as growing interest from public institutions. Participants included WWF offices from Peru, Colombia, and Brazil, government representatives from several regions of Peru (Amazonas, Uyacali, Madre de Dios), university researchers, MINAM, and government representatives from Ecuador and Brazil.

WWF Peru presented their ongoing analysis on the ecosystem service co-benefits of protecting jaguar habitat in Madre de Dios, and described the next steps in their analysis, including developing spatially-explicit scenarios for the region and assessing potential impacts on ecosystem services. Representatives from WWF Colombia and the Government of Acre, Brazil also presented their respective InVEST projects, the application of results to policy and decision-making, and lessons learned.
