InVEST in Practice

  • InVEST will be run on three major river basins in Patagonia to map the provision of ecosystem services, particularly as they relate to water resources, habitat quality, and biodiversity.

A Network for the Conservation of River Ecosystems of Patagonia


NatCap partners with the Patagonian National Center (CENPAT-CONICET) to create a technical research hub that will conduct ecosystem-level research on freshwater conservation. Our work is focused on three major rivers: the Chimehuin River in northwestern Patagonia, the Chubut River in central Patagonia, and the Rio Grande River in southern Patagonia.

Over five years, our network of research institutions will assess the costs and benefits associated with different water practices and develop management techniques to support balanced development. We hope to identify sustainable practices to conserve water resources and share our knowledge with the people of Patagonia. Our research also attempts to understand the link between healthy rivers and human well-being.

This project is particularly relevant for Patagonian people in context of recent population growth, urban development and the transformation of river basins through agricultural expansion. The last few free-flowing rivers are soon to be dammed, and mining activities are growing throughout the region. Many rivers and lakes are no longer able to sustain the recreational fisheries of the past, and freshwater access is increasingly limited in cities.

A Changing Landscape


To the right, a pair of photographs depict the Chimehuin River Valley. The top photograph was taken in 1970 and the bottom was taken from the same spot in 2012.

The striking differences between the photographs suggest a substantial change in vegetation, mostly associated with the invasion of exotic willows in riparian habitat and the addition of pine plantations. It is likely that these trees are affecting the hydrology, sediment retention, nutrient cycling, and even the morphology of the river itself. Our research will investigate the impacts of such changes and observe how ecological systems have changed over time, and how they might change in the future.
