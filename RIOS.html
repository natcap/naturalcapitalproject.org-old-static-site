Developing RIOS

In 2011 NatCap began developing RIOS in collaboration with the Latin American Water Funds Platform (a partnership among TNC, the Inter-American Development Bank, GEF, and FEMSA). We drew from the experiences of water fund practitioners and field scientists to create a widely applicable tool, directly relevant to those engaged in watershed investment

RIOS: Resource Investment Optimization System


RIOS is a free and open-source software tool designed to help identify cost-effective investments in watershed services. It provides a standardized, science-based approach to watershed management in contexts throughout the world. RIOS combines biophysical, social, and economic data to help users identify the best locations for protection and restoration activities in order to maximize the ecological return on investment, within the bounds of what is socially and politically feasible.


Generalized Tool, Flexible Approach


RIOS was developed through an extensive stakeholder engagement process, including input from more than 11 'water funds', or watershed investment programs, across Latin America. The tool has been tested in diverse ecological, social and political contexts. Depending on the context, RIOS-designed investments improved watershed service returns significantly (30%-600%) over alternative investment approaches. Having a tool like RIOS enables watershed investors to use a consistent, transparent, stakeholder-driven approach to evaluate projects within a region, or even between regions, making it easier to track the places where their investments are most needed and most effective. RIOS enables private entities, government agencies or non-government organizations to develop plans within the constraints of their budgets that maximize desired water benefits.


Questions RIOS can answer:

  • Which set of investments (in which activities, and where) will yield the greatest returns towards multiple water service objectives?
  • How much benefit is expected from making these set of investments identified through sicentific analysis
  • How much better are the estimated returns than what would have been achieved under 'business as usual' investments?

The RIOS software tool is:

Standardized. RIOS provides a replicable methodology of investing in the health of watersheds – humanity's natural defense against global environmental pressures. RIOS brings scientific rigor to investments made with real impact to nature and to people.

Flexible. RIOS can be applied in diverse environmental and socio political contexts. The software is capable of accommodating analyses of varying scale according to investors' budgets, desired outcomes, and available data.

Developed from the ground up. The design of RIOS was based on the experiences and needs of operating water funds in Latin America. It combines the input of scientists, watershed managers, and water fund stakeholders, resulting in a software that responds to real-world challenges in freshwater resources.
