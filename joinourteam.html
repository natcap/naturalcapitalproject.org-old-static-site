Coastal Engineer/Nearshore Oceanographer Research Assistant (Application review begins October 3, 2011)


The Nature Conservancy, World Wildlife Fund, the University of Minnesota, and Stanford University's Woods Institute together seek to fill the position of Coastal Engineer/Nearshore Oceanographer Research Assistant for the Natural Capital Project (NatCap). We seek a creative individual for a one and a half year position to support the development, documentation, and application of the Marine InVEST (Integrated Valuation of Ecosystem Services and Tradeoffs) models for coastal protection. Marine InVEST is a tool developed by NatCap to model and map the flow of multiple ecosystem services across seascapes.

The Research Assistant will be responsible for helping the Marine InVEST Coastal Protection model developers test and validate existing coastal erosion and inundation models, support the development of new models, and collate and summarize relevant literature. The Research Assistant will also help apply Marine InVEST in Galveston Bay, TX, Monterey Bay, CA, and in other locations in and outside the US. The Research Assistant may also be involved in the development and application of additional Marine InVEST models beyond the suite of coastal protection models.

This position requires wave, storm surge and/or nearshore circulation modeling skills. In addition, it requires good organizational skills, attention to detail, and an exceptional aptitude for teamwork.

The candidate should have strong independent research and analytical skills and excellent communication skills. Statistical experience is desired so that this person can help in developing functional relationships or parameterizations. InVEST is a tool within GIS. Basic experience with GIS is desirable, but not required.


Required Qualifications:


    • M.S. in civil engineering, coastal engineering, oceanography, or a similar field

    • Experience in wave and/or nearshore circulation modeling

    • Experience with data collection, data management and basic statistics

    • Good teamwork skills and demonstrated writing skills

    • Enjoys working in a highly collaborative environment

    • Ability to understand the big-picture of the Natural Capital Project Marine Initiative's work and to bring that understanding to bear on specific tasks


Term: one and a half years (with the potential for extension, contingent upon funding.)


Location: This position will be based with part of the NatCap Marine Initiative Team in Seattle, WA.


How to Apply: Application review will begin on Oct 3, 2011 and the position will be open until filled. To assure your application information is captured in our official files and that the hiring manager has immediate access to your resume and cover letter, you must apply to http://jobs.stanford.edu, Job #44637. A cover letter describing interest in and suitability for the position is required. Please include at the end of the cover letter contact information for 3 references.

Stanford University is committed to equal opportunity through affirmative action in employment and we are especially eager to identify minority persons and women with appropriate qualifications.
