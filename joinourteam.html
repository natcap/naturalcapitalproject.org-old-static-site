Software Engineer

The Software Engineer will support the development of existing and new software that the Natural Capital Project uses to assist policy makers in making science based decisions about environmental policies. This includes the InVEST (Integrated Valuation of Ecosystem Services and Tradeoffs) toolkit, RIOS (Resource Investment Optimization System) a land planning software suite, and a host of new online software components under development. Day to day development involves implementing mathematical models of environmental services with a focus on correctness, usability, and performance. Other traditional engineer job functions include developing test suites, implementing new features on existing models, user support, and supporting internal project management tools for the development team.

The successful candidate will be a friendly, intelligent, self-directed individual who is passionate about working on collaborative software projects for the social good.

GIS Programmer Analyst

We seek a creative and talented geographer or ecologist for a GIS programmer analyst position to advance the development, testing and application of terrestrial and freshwater ecosystem service models.

The successful candidate will contribute to NatCap's ongoing efforts to provide process-based ecosystem service modeling in support of decision making around the world. To date, these efforts have resulted in InVEST, a tool that tracks the production and valuation of ecosystem services, and RIOS, a landscape optimization support tool for watershed services. The programmer analyst will develop, program, improve and apply spatially-explicit ecosystem services models written in Python. In addition, they will gather, create and prepare digital data needed for GIS analysis. The programmer analyst will work in close collaboration with NatCap's interdisciplinary team, which includes scientists, economists and software engineers, to produce simple, scientifically robust models that have outputs that resonate with stakeholders and decision makers.

Our core team is located in Seattle, Washington, DC, University of Minnesota and Stanford, and we have active partners around the globe. This position will be based at Stanford. It will require extensive interaction with interdisciplinary teams and some travel.

For the full position description and to apply, visit Stanford University's Career Page, and search for Job Number 61879.Include a cover letter describing your experience and interest, as well as CV and contact information for three references. We will begin reviewing applications Feb 17, 2014 and the position will remain open until filled.


Economist Post-Doc

We seek a creative and talented economist for a post-doctoral research position to advance the development, testing and application of terrestrial and freshwater ecosystem service models.

The successful candidate will develop, expand, and improve economic models for ecosystem service valuation as part of NatCap's ongoing efforts to provide process-based ecosystem service modeling in support of decision making. To date, these efforts have resulted in InVEST, a spatially-explicit tool that tracks the production and valuation of ecosystem services, and RIOS, a landscape optimization support tool for watershed services. The postdoc will work in a large, interdisciplinary team to create and improve simple, scientifically-robust models that have outputs that resonate with stakeholders and decision makers.

Beyond general support for valuation work in InVEST and RIOS, the postdoc will work with partners at the Inter-American Development Bank to value the impacts of different road building projects, and also develop new valuation approaches for hydrologic service models in the context of a water fund in Kenya.

Our core team is based in Seattle, Washington, DC, University of Minnesota, and Stanford University, and we have active partners around the globe. This position will be based at either Stanford University or the University of Minnesota, and will be advised by Dr. Gretchen Daily and Dr. Stephen Polasky. It will require extensive interaction with interdisciplinary teams and some travel.


Required Qualifications

  • A Ph.D. in environmental economics or agricultural and natural resource economics, or a related field
  • Experience developing economic models in the context of natural resource and or land-use management
  • Experience conducting empirical analyses related to market and non-market valuation

Preferred Qualifications

  • Experience integrating economic models with other biological, physical, or social models
  • Experience working with collaborators from diverse backgrounds and capacity and interest in working with interdisciplinary teams
  • Excellent communication skills
  • GIS experience

Term: One-year and beyond, contingent upon funding.

Send application to Robert Griffin (rmgriff@stanford.edu) with "econ post-doc application" in the subject. We will begin reviewing applications December 1st and the position will remain open until filled.
