Demonstration of InVEST:

A Decision-support Tool for Valuing Nature

Monday, December 10, 2012 | 8:30am-4:30pm
Ft. Lauterdale, FL

In this workshop, we introduces InVEST and provided hands-on training. We focused on data preparation for multiple scenarios, implementation in ArcGIS (computer mapping software) and interpretation of model results.

The workshop began with an introduction to InVEST and a general overview of the diverse decision-making contexts in which it has been applied. Then we demonstrated the concept of exploring trade-offs in ecosystem services with an interactive mapping exercise. Next, we walked-through a typical InVEST model (e.g. Renewable energy from waves) using sample data to generate outputs (e.g. suitability maps for siting a wave energy facility and analyzing tradeoffs among other ocean uses). The training also included small breakout groups to introduce two additional models of interest to participants. In the final portion of the workshop, participants had the opportunity to work with organizers from the Natural Capital Project to analyze model results from real-world case studies.
