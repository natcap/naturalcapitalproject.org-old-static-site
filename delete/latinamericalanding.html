Latin America

Scientists at the Natural Capital Project lead several projects in Latin America to integrate ecosystem services accounting into development and conservation decisions. In diverse ecological contexts we are employing the InVEST and RIOS tools to support ecosystem modeling and analysis of how nature's benefits are impacted by management and land-use choices.

Our work contributes to coastal spatial planning at the national scale in Belize, informs permitting and mitigation schemes in Colombia and Peru, and supports cost-effective allocation of investments in water security throughout Colombia, Ecuador, Mexico, and elsewhere.


Our work in Latin America engages communities, policy bodies and the private sector to protect and secure natural capital for people.


Integrated Spatial Planning in Belize

With the assistance of NatCap and WWF, the Belizean government is developing an Integrated Coastal Zone Management Plan (ICZMP) to better manage competing uses and future threats to its shoreline. NatCap scientists oversaw a participatory process of drafting the ICZMP that engaged local stakeholders from the public, industry, and government in discussion of realistic development tradeoffs, as revealed with the use of spatially-explicit InVEST models.

Climate Adaptation and Mitigation Schemes in Colombia

NatCap and its partners are using InVEST tools in several regions of Colombia to inform the siting of new development projects, to design a standard framework for environmental permitting and mitigation schemes, and to direct investment in conservation activities using quantified information on ecosystem services.

Optimizing Investments in Nature with RIOS

Latin America is making a major effort to address water scarcity through development of a new conservation financing mechanism known as water funds. The goal of these funds is to improve the management of watersheds, the green infrastructure that supplies, regulates, and cleans water. Based on the lessons and best practices from across the region, NatCap developed a standardized decision-support tool to manage water fund investments that is applicable in diverse ecological and social contexts. We continue to support the design and management of water funds throughout the region in partnership with the Latin American Water Funds Partnership.
