Natural Capital Project Fellows Program


Program Description

The Natural Capital Project Fellows program is designed for senior researchers interested in pursuing research interests that align with the mission and programs of the Natural Capital Project. This highly-selective program provides mid-career researchers and professionals with the opportunity to work along side NatCap scientists for a few months. The Fellowship is designed to provide a self-directed and mutually beneficial learning environment, we hope to learn as much from you and your research as you do from us. It is intended for researchers who have an existing project to work on and a position to which they will return following this fellowship.*

Fellows accepted into the program will have a primary liaison from the NatCap's research staff, who will coordinate their work and foster mutual learning with other researchers and the broader NatCap team. Most Fellows work with NatCap at our main office at Stanford University, where they can attend regular NatCap meetings and talks, and have access to the Stanford University community and events. Towards the end of their fellowship, they will have the opportunity to give a seminar on their work and present it to the NatCap research community.


Foreign Fellows

Stanford University can sponsor the appropriate visas for accepted Fellows and their families.


Costs

The registration fees for the Fellows Program are between $250 and $1,000, depending on the work eligibility and visa needs of the fellow. Limited scholarships are available to cover registration fees.

Visa fees charged by the US government, living, travel, and other expenses are not included. Participants are responsible for arranging their own room and board. A list of resources will be provided upon acceptance into the program.


Application

Please send the following materials to fellows@naturalcapitalproject.org, with "Fellows Program" in the subject line:

  • A detailed CV
  • A paragraph or two describing the research that you will lead while at Stanford. Your research proposal should describe how your work relates to an existing NatCap project, define any outputs, and propose a time and duration for your visit.
  • A paragraph detailing how you intend to use the Fellows experience and research after the program ends
  • Source of financial support and health insurance (if accepted into the program, proof of funds will be required for appointment)
  • Justification for a scholarship, if one is requested

Lead Times

Please allow at least one month for your application to be considered. If your application is accepted, plan for another two months before the earliest start date.




*Fellows are required to sign Stanford's Patent and Copyright Agreement prior to the start of their program.
