Natural Capital Project Fellows Program


Program Description

The Natural Capital Project Fellows program is designed for senior researchers interested in guided research on the value of natural capital to human well-being, and methods for integrating those values into decision making.

Fellows accepted into the program will be welcomed onto the Stanford University campus, where they will be housed within the Natural Capital Project (NatCap) at Herrin Labs. They will attend regular Natural Capital Project meetings and talks, and have access to the Stanford University community and events. They will have a primary liaison from the NatCap's research staff, who will coordinate their work and foster mutual learning with other researchers and the broader NatCap team.

Towards the end of their fellowship, they will have the opportunity to give a seminar/webinar on their work for Stanford and present it to the NatCap research community.

This program is highly selective, and can only include a small number of resident fellows at any one time. It is intended for researchers who have an existing position to which they will return following this fellowship.*


Foreign Fellows

Stanford University can sponsor the appropriate visas for accepted Fellows and their families.


Costs

The registration fees for the Fellows Program are $5000 for the first month, and $2500-5000 for subsequent months, depending on the nature of the planned work. Limited scholarships are available for Fellows Program fees only.

Visa fees charged by the US government, living, travel or other expenses are not included. Participants are responsible for arranging their own room and board. A list of resources will be provided upon acceptance into the program.


Application

Please send the following materials to fellows@naturalcapitalproject.org, with "Fellows Program" in the subject line:

  • A detailed CV
  • A paragraph describing the intended research area and potential outputs
  • A paragraph detailing how you intend to use the Fellows experience and research after the program ends
  • Source of financial support and health insurance (if accepted into the program, proof of funds will be required for appointment)
  • Justification for a scholarship, if one is requested

Lead Times

Please allow at least one month for your application to be considered. If your application is accepted, plan for another two months before the earliest start date.




*Fellows are required to sign Stanford's Patent and Copyright Agreement prior to the start of their program.
