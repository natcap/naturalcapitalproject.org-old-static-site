The first tool we are developing models and maps the distribution of biodiversity and flow of ecosystem services across a landscape.

We seek a creative individual for a 1-year position to support application of our models with stakeholders in our demonstration sites, including watersheds in the Sierra Nevada region of California, the Eastern Arc Mountains in Tanzania, North Shore of O’ahu, Hawai’i, and the Upper Yangtze River basin in China.

Water Resources and Hydrology Modeler (Posted 07 November 2007)


Description: The Nature Conservancy, World Wildlife Fund, and The Woods Institute for the Environment at Stanford University together seek to fill the position of Water Resources and Hydrology Modeler for the Natural Capital Project.

Our mission is to align economic forces with conservation by developing tools that make incorporating natural capital into decisions easy; by demonstrating the power of these tools in important, contrasting places; and by engaging leaders globally.

The first tool we are developing models and maps the distribution and flow of ecosystem services across a landscape.

We are developing and testing the tool in the Willamette Valley in Oregon and we are initially applying our approach in demonstration sites, including watersheds in the Sierra Nevada region of California, the Eastern Arc Mountains in Tanzania, North Shore of O’ahu, Hawai’i, and the Upper Yangtze River basin in China.

We are developing methods and data to support the modeling of the following ecosystem services: carbon sequestration; pollination; recreation, tourism, and other cultural values; and water-related services such as flow regulation and provision of water quality.

We seek to understand the differences in water quantity, water resources allocation, timing, and quality resulting from changes in land use and climate, and to demonstrate the connections between services affected by changes in hydrologic regime.

We seek a water resources and hydrology modeler for a two-year position to assist the Natural Capital Project’s Lead Eco-hydrologist in development and calibration of water resources system and hydrology models.

The position is based at Stanford University and includes some travel.

Salary level is commensurate with experience.

This position is not an independent research or work position. Expertise is gained through application and interdisciplinary team collaboration.


Principal Responsibilities:

    • Work under direction and supervision of the Natural Capital Project Lead Ecohydrologist in developing, testing, and calibrating simple yet sophisticated process-based model routines for use globally, including in areas with limited data.
    • Participate in workshops and communicate with project staff and partners on water-related services and modeling needs.
    • Work with team to advance valuation of water-related services.
    • Assist in developing training and outreach materials for models and their application.
    • Assist in developing well written documentation and manuals of work for use by partners and by project implementation teams.

Required Qualifications:

    • An advanced degree in Hydrology, Water Resources Systems Engineering, Agricultural Engineering, or related field. Applicants with a BS will be considered if they have extensive professional experience calibrating and applying models.
    • Experience parameterizing and applying a regional hydrology model, and modeling water resources systems.
    • Strong computer skills: UNIX/LINUX, FORTRAN are a must. Other software skills, such as PYTHON are a plus.
    • Experience with water resources systems modeling, including irrigation allocations, water rights, and reservoir release operation.
    • Experience in identifying and calibrating for key hydrology processes, and confronting the associated issues of limited data and uncertainty.
    • Extensive experience working with ARCGIS / GRASS and spatial modeling for water-related processes.
    • Interest and desire to develop applied solutions for conservation of biodiversity and improvements in human well-being.
