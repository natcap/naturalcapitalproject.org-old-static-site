Vermont Natural Capital & InVEST Training

June 12-14, 2013
Burlington, VT
Cost: $600
More information
Registration: Sold out

The Natural Capital Project (NatCap) is holding an InVEST Training in Vermont on June 12-14, 2013. This course will teach participants how to use the InVEST software suite and ways to apply approaches and tools for ecosystem service valuation in decision-making. A preliminary program with an introduction to InVEST and the natural capital approach will be held on June 12 at the United States Society for Ecological Economics conference, which all registrants are encouraged to attend.



Bali Natural Capital & InVEST Training

InVEST and Sub-Global Assessment (SGA) Network Training Workshop
August 30th - September 1st, 2013
Ecosystem Service Partnership (ESP) Conference - Bali, Indonesia
Costs: $ 400
ESP Members* and NatCap Partners (WWF, TNC, UMN, & Stanford): $ 300
Student rate**: $ 200
More information and registration

This 2.5 day workshop will focus on tools for Integrated Ecosystem Assessment and Management including hands-on training with InVEST and lessons-learned from Sub-Global Assessment case studies. Organised jointly by the Natural Capital Project and the Sub-Global Assessment Network Secretariat (UNEP-WCMC & The Cropper Foundation).


* Only full ESP members are eligible for the discounted registration fee. Click here to become an ESP member

** For students unable to attend the workshop due to the event fee of $200, a few scholarships are available to allow attendance at a discounted rate. To apply, email Gregg Verutes before July 1st, with a brief explanation of why you need the scholarship, what you hope to get out of the training, and how you intend to use InVEST in the future.

England Natural Capital & InVEST Training

JOctober 14-18, 2013
Wallingford, Oxford, UK
Cost: One-day Introductory Course (max. 100 participants): £55
Week-long Intensive Course (includes Day1; max. 30 participants): £345
More information and registration

This UK InVEST training course is run through a collaboration between Wessex BESS project team at the NERC Centre for Ecology and Hydrology and the NCP team. This course is run on a not for profit basis and is kindly subsidised by the NERC BESS programme directorate. The course will be located at CEH Wallingford, Oxford, UK and comprises two parts:

  • A one-day 'Introduction to InVEST' workshop- for researchers and practioners who wish to gain a basic working knowledge of InVEST software
  • A week-long intensive training course- for users who seek in-depth understanding of the InVEST models and to consider their future development
