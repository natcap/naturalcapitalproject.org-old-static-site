Training Program


Our training program hosts both introductory and technical workshops throughout the world. We are excited to work with new and existing InVEST users by offering training to students, scientists, practitioners and other groups. A typical training workshop can offer a number of sessions, including: 1) an introduction to our tools and approaches, 2) model demonstrations and hands-on GIS practice, 3) innovation and collaboration discussions, and 4) small-group support with participant data.

This page contains a list of confirmed training events. Please check back frequently as we continue to add new opportunities!

InVEST and Sub-Global Assessment (SGA) Network Training Workshop

England Natural Capital & InVEST Training

September 12-13, 2013
Puerto Madryn, Argentina
Cost: $200
 More information and registration

The Natural Capital Project is having an ecosystem services and InVEST training in Patagonia in association with CENPAT. This training will focus heavily on freshwater issues, particularly relating to modeling ecosystem services in river basins of Patagonia. This 2 day workshop will introduce participants to the Natural Capital Project Appraoch and using ecosystem service information in decisions. It will also provide a hands-on training for NatCap's Integrated Valuation of Ecosystem Services and Tradeoffs (InVEST) software suite, how it works and its capabilities and limitations. By the end of the workshop, participants should be able to run selected models, troubleshoot and use InVEST to compare scenarios and weigh trade-offs across different services.



England Natural Capital & InVEST Training

October 14-18, 2013
Wallingford, Oxford, UK
Cost: One-day Introductory Course (max. 100 participants): £55
Week-long Intensive Course (includes Day1; max. 30 participants): £345
More information and registration

This UK InVEST training course is run through a collaboration between Wessex BESS project team at the NERC Centre for Ecology and Hydrology and the NCP team. This course is run on a not for profit basis and is kindly subsidised by the NERC BESS programme directorate. The course will be located at CEH Wallingford, Oxford, UK and comprises two parts:

  • A one-day 'Introduction to InVEST' workshop- for researchers and practioners who wish to gain a basic working knowledge of InVEST software
  • A week-long intensive training course- for users who seek in-depth understanding of the InVEST models and to consider their future development
